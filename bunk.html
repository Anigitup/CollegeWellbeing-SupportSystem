Embrace Education

Skip Mass Bunks, Embrace Knowledge

Giving in to the temptation of mass bunking classes for fun may seem harmless at first. But regularly missing lectures and lessons leads to gaps in your knowledge that compound over time. Approach

Layer 1 - Emailing to College Dean

Send emails directly to the college dean/administration reporting unethical incidents like ragging. This gets the complaint directly to someone with authority to take action.

Advantages:

  • Creates a record of the complaint
  • Bypasses bureaucracy of formal procedures
  • Quick and easy way to report misconduct
  • Can be anonymous

Emailing the dean raises awareness at a senior level. It signals students are watching and won't tolerate unethical practices.


Layer 2 - Counselor Support

Connect affected students with professional counselors for emotional healing and empowerment.

  • Professional Counseling: Confidential sessions for trauma processing.
  • Peer Support: Encouragement through peer-led groups.
  • Workshops: Boost confidence and manage trauma.

Advantages:

  • Prevents Escalation: Early intervention curbs long-term effects.
  • Strengthened Resilience: Equips coping strategies for stress.
  • Fosters Unity: Peer support builds community.

Layer 3 - Student Support

Connect students affected by ragging/harassment with counselors and resources like:

  • Professional counseling
  • Academic advising
  • Peer support groups
  • Workshops on confidence and trauma

Advantages:

  • Helps students process and recover
  • Reduces chances of dropping out
  • Empowers students to speak out
  • Fosters community and support
  • Shows the college cares about student wellbeing
